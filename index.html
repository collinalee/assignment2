Key Concepts

Brand vs Product
  • Product is tangible.
  • Brand is intangible.

Elements of a Brand
  1. Brand Name - Spoken name of a brand / communicates attributes.
  2. URL - Reflects a company in its fullness / the web address for a company.
  3. Logo/Symbol - a logo has letters, and is the written representation of a brand / a symbol is an image with no text that represents a brand.
  4. Slogan - a short phrase that describes a brand's attributes.
  5. Character - mascot of a brand (ex. Flo from Progressive) / NOT a spokesperson
  6. Jingle - short musical piece that reflects the positioning of a brand.

FMCG (CPG) = Fast Moving Consumer Goods:
  • Goods are bought frequently.
  • High turnover.
  • Relatively low prices.
  • Mostly non-durable goods (they don't last a long time/you use them up quickly).
  • There are both perishable and non-perishable CPG items.
  • Low margins with high volume.

CPG from the Consumer's Perspective:
  • Steady flow of recurring expenses.
  • CPG items are frequent purchases because they are consumed quickly.
  • Low consumer involvement.
  • Buy -> Consume -> Purchase.

CPG from the Company's Perspective:
  • High Volume / Low Margins.
  • Intense distribution (# of categories and points of sale).

The 3 Musketeers of CPG

All go up or down together.

  1. Price
  2. Risk
  3. Infrequency of Purchase

CPG Company Challenges:
  • External:
    • Low barriers to entry.
    • Fierce competition.
    • Slowing sales growth.
    • Rapidly changing wants & tastes / consumer boredom.
    • Low switching costs.
    • Must prevent brand dilution/erosion.
  • Internal:
    • Constant cost-containment concerns.
    • Productivity Tools:
      • Sales CRM.
      • Automation.
      • Economies of Scale.
